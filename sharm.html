Sharm-El-Shaikh

More Informaion about Sharm-El-Shaikh

Sharm El-Sheikh is one of Egypt's most popular tourist destinations, located at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula along the Red Sea. Known for its stunning beaches, vibrant marine life, luxurious resorts, and an array of outdoor activities, Sharm El-Sheikh offers something for every kind of traveler—whether you're interested in relaxation, adventure, or culture.

Here's an overview of Sharm El-Sheikh and the activities you can enjoy there:

Top Activities in Sharm-El-Shaikh:

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling
    • Sharm El-Sheikh is considered one of the world's best diving destinations due to its clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and abundant marine
    Ras Mohammed National Park and Tiran Island are among the best spots for diving, where you can explore the colorful coral reefs, swim with tropical fish, and even encounter sharks and turtles.
    If you're not a certified diver, there are plenty of snorkeling trips available that provide you with the chance to experience the underwater world from the surface.

  • Beach Relaxation and Water Sports
    • Sharm El-Sheikh boasts numerous sandy beaches where you can enjoy some relaxation in the sun. Popular beaches include Naama Bay and Shark's Bay. Apart from lounging by the sea, you can also engage in water sports like windsurfing, kite surfing, parasailing, and banana boat rides.
    -Jet skiing and stand-up paddleboarding are also popular along the coast.

  • Desert Safari
    • The desert surrounding Sharm El-Sheikh offers a stark contrast to the beach areas and provides exciting opportunities for adventure. You can explore the Sinai desert in a 4x4 jeep, on camels, or by quad biking
    Many desert safari tours include a visit to Bedouin camps where you can experience local culture, enjoy traditional meals, and stargaze under the clear desert sky.

  • Nightlife and Dining
    • Sharm El-Sheikh is known for its vibrant nightlife, with many clubs, bars, and restaurants offering live music, DJ sets, and traditional performances. The city has a wide range of international cuisine, including seafood, Mediterranean, and Middle Eastern
    Some popular nightlife spots include the Hard Rock Cafe, Pacha Sharm El Sheikh, and Little Buddha.

  • Glass-Bottom Boat Tours
    • If you prefer to stay dry but still want to explore the underwater world, glass-bottom boat tours offer a great way to see the marine life without getting in the water. These tours are ideal for families, children, or those who don't want to snorkel or dive.

  • Spas and Wellness
    • Sharm El-Sheikh is home to a number of luxurious spas and wellness centers where you can relax with traditional treatments like massage, aromatherapy, and mud baths.
    Many of the high-end resorts offer spa services that overlook the Red Sea, allowing you to unwind in a tranquil setting.

  • Shopping
    • Sharm El-Sheikh has a variety of shopping options, ranging from local bazaars to high-end malls. You can find Egyptian handicrafts, jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs in the bustling markets such as Old Market and Naama Bay.
    Leather goods, papyrus paintings, and spices are popular items to take home.

  • Camel Rides
    • Camel rides are a traditional activity in the desert areas surrounding Sharm El-Sheikh. You can take a camel ride at sunset for a picturesque experience as you explore the desert landscape.

    Best Time to Visit:

    • The winter months (October to April)
      • The best time to visit, when the temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities like diving, sightseeing, and desert safaris. Temperatures range from 18°C to 25°C (64°F to 77°F) during these months.
    • The summer months (June to September)
      • can be very hot, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F), making it less ideal for outdoor exploration but still perfect for beach vacations.
